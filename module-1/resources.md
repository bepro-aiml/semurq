# Module 1: Introduction to AI and ML — Learning Resources

Curated resources aligned with lecture sections. Complete all **Required** items before the next session.

---

### 1. Introduction to AI and ML (Sections 1-3)

**Videos:**

- **Google ML Crash Course** — "What is ML?" and "Framing" modules (~1h) [Beginner] **Required**
  https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/crash-course
  Covers core definitions, supervised vs unsupervised framing, and what makes a good ML problem.

- **Google Introduction to AI** on Coursera (~4h, free audit) [Beginner] **Required**
  https://www.coursera.org/learn/google-introduction-to-ai
  Broad overview of AI concepts, terminology, and real-world applications.

- **Elements of AI** by University of Helsinki — Chapters 1-3 (~6h) [Beginner] **Optional**
  https://www.elementsofai.com/
  Interactive course. Good for students who want a slower, more thorough introduction.

**Readings:**

- **Microsoft ML-For-Beginners** — Lessons 1-4: Intro, History, Fairness, Techniques [Beginner] **Required**
  https://github.com/microsoft/ML-For-Beginners
  Well-structured GitHub curriculum with code examples and quizzes.

- **Google ML Problem Framing** guide [Beginner] **Optional**
  https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/problem-framing
  How to decide whether ML is the right approach and how to frame the problem.
