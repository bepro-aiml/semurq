Module 1: Introduction to AI and ML — Learning Resources

Curated resources aligned with lecture sections. Complete all Required items before the next session.

1. Introduction to AI and ML (Sections 1-3)

Videos:

  • Google ML Crash Course — “What is ML?” and “Framing” modules (~1h) [Beginner] Required https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/crash-course Covers core definitions, supervised vs unsupervised framing, and what makes a good ML problem.

  • Google Introduction to AI on Coursera (~4h, free audit) [Beginner] Required https://www.coursera.org/learn/google-introduction-to-ai Broad overview of AI concepts, terminology, and real-world applications.

  • Elements of AI by University of Helsinki — Chapters 1-3 (~6h) [Beginner] Optional https://www.elementsofai.com/ Interactive course. Good for students who want a slower, more thorough introduction.

Readings:

  • Microsoft ML-For-Beginners — Lessons 1-4: Intro, History, Fairness, Techniques [Beginner] Required https://github.com/microsoft/ML-For-Beginners Well-structured GitHub curriculum with code examples and quizzes.

  • Google ML Problem Framing guide [Beginner] Optional https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/problem-framing How to decide whether ML is the right approach and how to frame the problem.

2. Types of Machine Learning (Section 4)

Videos:

  • StatQuest — “Machine Learning Fundamentals” (~10min) [Beginner] Required https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLblh5JKOoLUICTaGLRoHQDuF_7q2GfuJF Clear visual explanation of supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.

3. Theoretical Foundations (Section 5)

Videos:

  • 3Blue1Brown — “But what is a neural network?” (~20min) [Beginner] Required https://www.3blue1brown.com/topics/neural-networks Builds intuition for optimization and gradient descent through visual math.

  • StatQuest — “Probability is not Likelihood” and “Bayes’ Theorem” [Beginner] Optional https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLblh5JKOoLUICTaGLRoHQDuF_7q2GfuJF Foundational probability concepts. Watch if you need a refresher on stats basics.

4. ITES Applications (Section 6)

No specific videos required — this is a discussion-based section.

Assignment: - Research one real AI application in Uzbekistan (e-commerce, telecom, banking, or government). - Prepare a 2-minute verbal summary: what problem it solves, what type of ML it likely uses.
