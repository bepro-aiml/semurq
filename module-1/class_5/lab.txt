## The Real Point of Today

In your career, you will rarely be told "build this ML model." You will be told "we have a business problem, figure it out." The single most important skill is **researching the problem before writing any code**. Today you practice that.

You will NOT build a model today. You will not write code. You will research, think, frame, and defend your approach. This is what senior ML engineers spend 80% of their time doing.

## How to Do Research (Read This First)

Your research process for every scenario:

**Step 1: Understand the business (5 min)**
- Read the scenario twice
- Write down what the company actually wants to achieve
- Write down who will use the result and how

**Step 2: Find real examples (10 min)**
- Search for companies that solved similar problems
- Look for case studies, blog posts, research papers
- Use the links provided in your scenario as starting points
- Follow citations and related links

**Step 3: Frame the ML problem (10 min)**
- Is this supervised, unsupervised, or reinforcement?
- Is the output a number (regression) or category (classification)?
- What data would you need?
- What features matter?

**Step 4: Check the data reality (10 min)**
- Where would the data actually come from in Uzbekistan?
- What would be hard to collect?
- What privacy or legal issues exist?

**Step 5: Consider the ethics (5 min)**
- Who benefits from this model?
- Who could be harmed?
- What decision would you escalate to a human?

**Step 6: Present (5 min per group)**
- 3-minute presentation
- 2-minute Q&A from other groups and mentor

## SCENARIO 1 — Uztelecom Customer Churn

**The business problem:**
Uztelecom is losing 3-5% of subscribers every month. Management wants to predict who will leave in the next 30 days so the retention team can offer deals before customers cancel.

**Your research mission:**
Frame this as an ML problem. Define what "churn" actually means. List what data you would need. Consider privacy and fairness.

**Start your research here:**
- Kaggle Telco Churn dataset (look at feature names): https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/blastchar/telco-customer-churn
- Customer churn industry article: https://towardsdatascience.com/hands-on-predict-customer-churn-5c2a42806266
- StatQuest Logistic Regression: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yIYKR4sgzI8
- Uztelecom public info: https://uztelecom.uz
- Research paper on churn: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=telecom+customer+churn+prediction

**Hard questions to answer:**
- Does "churn" mean they canceled the contract, or just stopped using data?
- A prepaid customer who did not top up in 60 days — churned or not?
- If you flag low-income users as high churn risk and the retention team calls them aggressively, is that ethical?
- What is the class imbalance? (Hint: not 50/50)

**What to submit:**
- Your full framing template (business objective, target, ML type, 10+ features, evaluation metric, class imbalance, ethics, data availability)
- 3 links you used in your research

## SCENARIO 2 — Uzum Marketplace Product Recommendations

**The business problem:**
Uzum wants to increase sales by showing each customer products they are likely to buy. Currently, the homepage shows the same products to everyone.

**Your research mission:**
Design a recommendation system. Decide between content-based filtering, collaborative filtering, or a hybrid approach. Handle the cold-start problem for new users.

**Start your research here:**
- Netflix recommendation algorithm explained: https://research.netflix.com/research-area/recommendations
- Collaborative filtering basics: https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/recommendation
- Towards Data Science recommendation guide: https://towardsdatascience.com/recommendation-systems-explained-a42fc60591ed
- YouTube algorithm explainer: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BSpAWkQLlgM
- Uzum marketplace: https://uzum.uz

**Hard questions to answer:**
- How do you recommend products to a brand new user with zero history? (cold start)
- How do you balance showing popular items vs diverse items?
- What happens if the algorithm creates filter bubbles (only shows similar products)?
- How do you measure success — clicks, purchases, or something else?

**What to submit:**
- Framing template
- Sketch of how the system would work (data flow)
- How you would handle cold start
- 3 links used

## SCENARIO 3 — Fraud Detection for Payme / Click

**The business problem:**
Mobile payment platforms process millions of transactions daily. A tiny fraction are fraudulent — stolen cards, account takeovers, money laundering. Fraud must be caught in real-time, in under 1 second.

**Your research mission:**
Design a fraud detection system. Decide between supervised classification, unsupervised anomaly detection, or a hybrid. Handle the severe class imbalance (fraud is less than 0.1% of transactions).

**Start your research here:**
- Kaggle Credit Card Fraud dataset (read the feature descriptions): https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/mlg-ulb/creditcardfraud
- Fraud detection in banking: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xoI6M4RSKmw
- Anomaly detection methods: https://scikit-learn.org/stable/modules/outlier_detection.html
- SMOTE for imbalanced data: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FheTB3Y7bAk
- Payme: https://payme.uz

**Hard questions to answer:**
- If the model blocks a legitimate transaction, what happens to the customer? Is that worse than missing fraud?
- How do you handle concept drift (fraudsters change tactics every week)?
- What features can you compute in 500 milliseconds? (transaction velocity, geographic distance from usual)
- Is it ethical to flag transactions based on location? What about ethnicity (inferred from name)?

**What to submit:**
- Framing template
- Evaluation metric choice defended (precision vs recall tradeoff)
- How you would handle class imbalance
- 3 links used

## SCENARIO 4 — Cotton Crop Yield Prediction in Fergana Valley

**The business problem:**
Uzbekistan is a major cotton producer. A company wants to predict cotton yield per hectare 3 months before harvest, based on satellite imagery, weather data, and soil data. Accurate predictions help with export contracts and pricing.

**Your research mission:**
Frame this as an ML problem. Decide what data to use. Think about how to validate predictions on farms you have never seen.

**Start your research here:**
- NASA satellite data for agriculture: https://data.nasa.gov
- Kaggle agriculture datasets: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets?tags=agriculture
- Remote sensing with ML: https://towardsdatascience.com/remote-sensing-for-beginners-10f7094dfa7c
- Google Earth Engine (free for research): https://earthengine.google.com
- Research paper: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=cotton+yield+prediction+satellite

**Hard questions to answer:**
- Is this a regression problem (predict a number) or classification (high/medium/low yield)?
- How do you handle years with unusual weather (drought, floods)?
- What is the unit of prediction — per hectare, per farm, per region?
- Who benefits from accurate predictions? Who loses?

**What to submit:**
- Framing template
- Data sources you would combine
- How you would test the model (temporal vs spatial split)
- 3 links used

## SCENARIO 5 — Hospital Patient Triage in Samarkand

**The business problem:**
A regional hospital in Samarkand wants to predict which emergency room patients need urgent care versus those who can wait. Currently, a nurse makes this judgment in under 2 minutes. An ML assistant could help — but it must NEVER delay critical care.

**Your research mission:**
Frame this as an ML problem. This is high-stakes — a wrong answer can kill someone. Think hard about human oversight and failure modes.

**Start your research here:**
- Clinical decision support systems: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-020-00341-z
- MIMIC-III medical dataset (free with registration): https://physionet.org/content/mimiciii/1.4/
- Medical AI ethics: https://www.coursera.org/learn/ethics-ai
- Healthcare AI case study: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=healthcare+AI+triage
- Research papers: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=emergency+triage+machine+learning

**Hard questions to answer:**
- What happens when the model is wrong and a patient dies?
- Should the model be autonomous or advisory to a nurse?
- How do you handle patients whose symptoms the model has never seen?
- Is it ethical to use this in rural areas where hospitals are understaffed?
- Privacy — medical data is extremely sensitive

**What to submit:**
- Framing template
- Human oversight plan (when does a human override the model?)
- Failure modes and mitigations
- 3 links used

## SCENARIO 6 — Tashkent Metro Traffic Flow Optimization

**The business problem:**
Tashkent metro is expanding. The city wants to predict rider demand at each station for every hour of the day so they can adjust train frequency. Fewer trains at low-demand times saves electricity and wear. More trains at high-demand times reduces crowding.

**Your research mission:**
Frame this as an ML problem. Decide between time-series forecasting, clustering, or reinforcement learning. Consider how to handle special events (holidays, concerts, weather).

**Start your research here:**
- Time series forecasting with ML: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e8Yw4alG16Q
- Kaggle time series datasets: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets?search=transportation
- Traffic prediction research: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=metro+passenger+flow+prediction
- Google Transit data: https://developers.google.com/transit
- Tashkent metro info: https://metro.tashkent.uz

**Hard questions to answer:**
- Is this supervised (predict a number) or reinforcement (optimize train schedule)?
- What features matter? (time of day, day of week, weather, holidays, school schedule)
- How do you predict demand for a new station that just opened?
- Can this model accidentally encourage surveillance of riders?

**What to submit:**
- Framing template
- Time granularity (minutes, hours, days?)
- How you would handle special events
- 3 links used

## Presentation Format (30 min total)

Each group gets 5 minutes:
- 3 minutes to present their framing
- 2 minutes for questions from other groups and the mentor

Structure your presentation:
1. What is the problem? (20 seconds)
2. What did you find in research? (60 seconds)
3. How did you frame it? (60 seconds)
4. What are the ethical risks? (30 seconds)
5. What would you do differently in Uzbekistan? (30 seconds)

---

## Mentor's Tips (Share With Groups)

- If your group can define "churn" or "fraud" in under 2 minutes, you did not think hard enough. The definition IS the problem.
- Beware of obvious features like "monthly spending." Any junior data analyst can think of those. What would a senior ML engineer look for? (Hint: changes over time, ratios, behavioral patterns)
- Always ask "what happens when this model is wrong?" — not "what happens when it is right?"
- If you cannot get the data in Uzbekistan, your model is useless here. Think locally.
